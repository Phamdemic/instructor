Week 2

Goal

Explore the class project data, both in its native format and in a database

Objectives

Explore the Kindergarten Lottery data in a spreadsheet
Import the data into a database
Explore the Kindergarten Lottery data in a database

Details

1. first download the following:
http://onyx.csit.parkland.edu/~csc191/data/u4_lottery_data.xlsx
http://onyx.csit.parkland.edu/~csc191/sql/create_lottery.sql
http://onyx.csit.parkland.edu/~csc191/data/u4_lottery_data.csv

2. Explore the data (xlsx) visually with Excel (or another spreadsheet program):

  • how many total people?
  • how many people live in Urbana? Savoy? Champaign? Other cities?
  • what does "preasgcd" mean?
  • how many different columns are there?
  • how many people chose BO as their 3rd choice school (ch3schna)?

3. Load data (csv) into a table.
For mysql/phpmyadmin, you will probably want to create a new database (I use u4lottery). I imported the file into the default table ("table 1"), and then renamed the table. For sqlite, you might want to create a new database as well (u4lottery?) and do the same with the table.

BETTER INSTRUCTIONS
For those struggling to comprehend my totally unclear and cryptic instructions, try this:

  1. in phpMyAdmin, select a table on the left-hand side
  2. Click the "Import" tab
  3. Click the "Browse your computer" radio button, then click "Choose File"
  4. Navigate to wherever you downloaded create_lottery.sql, open it
  5. Click "Go" (near the bottom)
  6. Click on the new "lottery" table on the left-hand side
  7. Click the "Browse your computer" radio button, the click "Choose File"
  8. Navigate to wherever you downloaded u4_lottery_data.csv, open it
  9. Click "Go" (near the bottom)
  10. Wait for a minute
If that doesn't work for you, please let me know.

4. Explore the data via sql:

  • how many total people?
  • how many people live in Urbana? Savoy? Champaign? Other cities?
  • how many different columns are there?
  • how many people chose BO as their 3rd choice school (ch3schna)?
  • of the people that chose BO as their 1st choice (ch1schna), how many were not assigned to BO (asgsch)? HINT: BO is number school# 10.
  • how many people did not make a 5th choice (ch5schna)? HINT: check for blank values, but not NULL *grin*
  • how many people were not assigned to a school?

    • 5. Your turn; come up with two of your own questions (doesn't matter how complex). Can you think of queries to answer the questions?
